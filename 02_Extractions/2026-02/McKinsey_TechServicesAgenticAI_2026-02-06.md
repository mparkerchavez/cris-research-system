## Initial Observations

The McKinsey analysis presents a critical inflection point for the technology services industry. While agentic AI creates significant disruption risk—potentially compressing core services revenue by 20-30%—it simultaneously opens new $100-400 billion opportunity pools in business function transformation and $200 billion in agentic workflow services. The paradox is that most enterprises, despite 80% running pilots and 50% planning scaled deployments, struggle to convert agentic investments into material earnings impact. This gap between adoption momentum and realized value creates the precise conditions where tech services providers can position themselves as essential partners, shifting from execution-focused vendors to strategic enablers of human-agent operating models.

The competitive landscape is rapidly fragmenting as traditional boundaries blur between software vendors, hyperscalers, pure-play AI companies, and services providers. Successful tech services players must simultaneously operate across four distinct value propositions—from foundational agentic enablers to end-to-end workflow disruptors—while mastering a fundamentally different commercial model. The shift from time-and-materials pricing toward outcome-based, subscription, and gain-share models reflects a deeper transformation: enterprises are increasingly sophisticated about measuring AI impact and demanding clear accountability, making human-agent collaboration design and governance capabilities as critical as technical depth.

The research underscores an emerging execution bottleneck that extends beyond technology. The challenges of integrating agents with legacy systems pale against the organizational change management, talent reskilling, and cultural alignment required to realize the promised 15-30% workforce productivity gains. The providers that win will be those who understand agentic AI not as a technological upgrade but as an operational and organizational transformation requiring deep domain knowledge, rapid prototyping partnerships, and the ability to manage the complex handoffs between human and agent work at scale.
