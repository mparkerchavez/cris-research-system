## Executive Summary

This report analyzes 5,124 AI use cases from 1,245 practitioners across 11 industries, conducted October-November 2025. The study measures real ROI data from practitioners and reveals measurable enterprise value from AI deployment. Key finding: 81.8% of companies report positive ROI today, with 95.7% expecting positive ROI within 12 months.

## Initial Observations

**Data Quality & Methodology:** This survey represents a large practitioner testimonial (5,124 use cases) but carries significant biases: self-selection toward engaged users, 25% small business overrepresentation, and self-reported rather than audited ROI metrics. These limitations are clearly acknowledged by authors and should be weighted in synthesis.

**ROI Momentum Pattern:** The 81.8% → 95.7% current-to-expected ROI arc reflects either genuine organizational confidence or post-investment rationalization bias. The shift in median expectation from "Modest" to "Significant" suggests respondents believe they're entering a compounding phase after initial investment.

**Size-Advantage Complexity:** The 19% ROI advantage for small companies (3.49 vs 2.94) is substantial but requires caution. Explanations offered (agility, fewer constraints) are plausible, but the data cannot isolate whether small company advantage is structural, selection-biased, or measurement-biased.

**Strategic vs Operational Bifurcation:** The finding that strategic benefits (decision-making, new capabilities) correlate with higher ROI while time savings alone correlates lower is important. This supports the "growth ROI vs efficiency ROI" distinction referenced in recent briefings but needs verification against broader samples.

**Tool Specificity Pattern:** Only 14.5% of use cases mention specific tools, with clear differentiation: generic tools → efficiency, specialized tools → strategic value. This aligns with vendor-agnostic positioning but suggests enterprise tool selection is driven by outcome requirements, not brand loyalty.

**Automation Adoption Reality:** 13.8% agentic AI adoption is significantly lower than recent hype suggests. The mid-market sweet spot (51-200 employees at 17.6% adoption) and VP-level concentration (19.7%) reveal that agent deployment is top-down, not organic. Regulated industries outperform in adoption (18.2%), contradicting narrative about regulatory drag.

**Industry Narrative Inversion:** Knowledge-intensive sectors (Government 86, Education 76, Media 71) reporting 2-3x benefits over Finance (25) and Healthcare (40) challenges stereotypes about public sector and education tech lag. Likely reflects that information work is more readily transformed by AI than process-heavy, compliance-driven work.
