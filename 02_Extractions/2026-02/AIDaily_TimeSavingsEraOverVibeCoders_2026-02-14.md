## Initial Observations

The shift from time savings to output multiplication and new capabilities marks a fundamental transition from AI-as-efficiency-tool to AI-as-capability-expansion. This distinction has profound implications for organizational ROI measurement frameworks and workforce strategy. The data reveals that skill development and learning velocity are now the binding constraints on AI value realization, not tool availability or cost. The 49.5% of non-engineers performing vibe coding suggests that coding competency is transitioning from specialized expertise to general professional capability, with significant implications for organizational design, hiring, and role definition. The C-suite's disproportionate agentic adoption (57% vs general population ~38%) suggests top-down awareness of transformative potential, yet organizational policies remain restrictive, creating a governance-readiness gap.
