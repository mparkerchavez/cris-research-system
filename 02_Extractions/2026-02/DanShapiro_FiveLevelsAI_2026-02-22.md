## Initial Observations

This piece provides a practical framework for understanding AI development maturity beyond hype cycles. The five-level model maps cognitive transitions: from tool-user to code-reviewer to specification-author to automator. The psychological inflection at Level Two (false completion) and Level Three (perceived regression in role) suggest adoption barriers are not purely technical but identity-based. The author positions Level Four and Five as achievable with small teams, implying scale constraints differ fundamentally from traditional software organizations. The Dark Factory reference carries implicit concern about organizational transformation endpoint — suggesting that full automation may displace rather than augment human roles.
