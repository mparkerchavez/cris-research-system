## Atomic Data Points

**DP1:** Rapid viral growth for AI products creates churn that damages feedback loops required for product improvement, particularly harmful for agent-native applications

- **Anchor:** "When a surge of one-time users arrives and quickly abandons, the churn starves the system of the feedback loops it needs to improve"
- **Citation:** (para. 3)
- **Tags:** #product-strategy, #activation-gap, #user-experience-acceleration

**DP2:** AI products depend on repeated interaction and relationship depth over viral reach, reversing entertainment industry growth logic

- **Anchor:** "They need the same users to return again and again—enough for the AI model or agent to learn them and vice versa"
- **Citation:** (para. 3)
- **Tags:** #product-strategy, #use-case-development, #measurement-framework-reckoning

**DP3:** Agent-native applications enable non-linear, unpredictable use cases that cannot be compressed into single viral moments or demo sequences

- **Anchor:** "AI apps, especially agent-native ones, tend to be non-linear, unpredictable, and sometimes have a virtually infinite number of use cases"
- **Citation:** (para. 5)
- **Tags:** #agent-native-development, #product-strategy, #specification-bottleneck

**DP4:** Effective AI product marketing emphasizes multiple small realizations ("aha" moments) rather than single transformative moments

- **Anchor:** "What you're really optimizing for here isn't a single 'wow' moment but a series of small 'ahas'"
- **Citation:** (para. 5)
- **Tags:** #product-strategy, #user-experience-acceleration, #adoption-barriers

**DP5:** Building in public for AI products serves dual purpose of marketing and user education about patterns, capabilities, and expected outcomes

- **Anchor:** "By building in public, you're simultaneously marketing the product and teaching people how to work with it, what kinds of questions to ask, and what good outcomes look like"
- **Citation:** (para. 6)
- **Tags:** #product-strategy, #workforce-readiness, #skill-formation

**DP6:** Traditional software metrics (session length, time to value) misfit agent systems where meaningful value moments are qualitative and contextual

- **Anchor:** "Traditional metrics like session length, sessions per user, or 'time to value' were designed for software with fixed flows. The meaningful moments are often qualitative, unexpected, and highly contextual"
- **Citation:** (para. 7)
- **Tags:** #measurement-framework-reckoning, #agentic-workflows, #product-strategy

**DP7:** Direct user communication creates co-creation narrative that builds trust sufficient to retain users through agent failure and learning cycles

- **Anchor:** "Showing up personally to those conversations does something else. It turns your product into a shared story, one in which users feel like they're co-creating the product with you"
- **Citation:** (para. 7)
- **Tags:** #product-strategy, #trust-fairness, #human-centered-ai

**DP8:** For AI products, relationship effects (personalization, memory, trust) create greater value than network effects, requiring extended user tenure

- **Anchor:** "For consumer AI apps, what's even more important than network effects—where your product gets more valuable as more people use it—is relationship effects"
- **Citation:** (para. 8)
- **Tags:** #product-strategy, #use-case-development, #trust-fairness

**DP9:** Pre-activation strategy — building relationships with users before signup — creates stronger product-market fit than post-download conversion optimization

- **Anchor:** "My big bet for 2026 is that for an AI app to succeed it will need to 'pre-activate' users—to have already built that relationship with them before they sign up and download it"
- **Citation:** (para. 8)
- **Tags:** #product-strategy, #activation-gap, #market-demand

**DP10:** AI product success requires trading virality for retained, pre-educated users who understand product capabilities through prior exposure

- **Anchor:** "If you need to trade virality for these kinds of users, so be it. For a shot at building a truly durable consumer AI product, it's a trade worth making"
- **Citation:** (para. 9)
- **Tags:** #product-strategy, #market-demand, #activation-gap

**DP11:** Marketing AI products should showcase everyday mundane interactions rather than peak transformation moments to build accurate user expectations

- **Anchor:** "You don't want to show one most impressive thing. You want to show the boring but recognizable moments potential customers experience every day"
- **Citation:** (para. 5)
- **Tags:** #product-strategy, #marketing, #user-experience-acceleration

**DP12:** Emergent capabilities — unexpected use cases discovered by users rather than defined by developers — represent core value proposition for agent-native products

- **Anchor:** "Ideally all your growth efforts would empower users to discover what Every cofounder and CEO Dan Shipper calls 'emergent capabilities'—things the product can accomplish for the user that weren't explicitly defined by the developer"
- **Citation:** (para. 5)
- **Tags:** #agent-native-development, #product-strategy, #specification-bottleneck
