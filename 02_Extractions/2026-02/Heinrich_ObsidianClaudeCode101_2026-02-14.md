## Initial Observations

Heinrich's vault system represents a conceptual breakthrough: treating knowledge work as analogous to software engineering, with agents as first-class system operators rather than tools. The architecture inverts traditional personal knowledge management: instead of humans organizing then consulting, humans establish system principles while agents perform organization, discovery, and synthesis. The key innovation is treating notes as composable code-like artifacts with explicit linking semantics and agent-readable convention layers. This extends agent-native development beyond engineering to epistemic domains, suggesting a broader pattern where agent-aware structure (markdown, linking, metadata, instruction files) enables both human and machine reasoning. The emphasis on personal philosophy encoded in CLAUDE.md files suggests that agent-native systems require explicit articulation of otherwise-implicit human judgment frameworks.
