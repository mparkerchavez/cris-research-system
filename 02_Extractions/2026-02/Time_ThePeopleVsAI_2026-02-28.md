## Data Points

**DP1:** Public opposition to AI development now spans political ideologies and geographic regions, forming rare consensus.
- **Anchor:** "from MAGA loyalists to Democratic socialists, pastors to policymakers, nurses to filmmakers—agree on at least one thing: AI is moving too fast"
- **Citation:** (para. 3)
- **Tags:** #competitive-disruption, #policy-gap

**DP2:** American public sentiment on AI is distinctly pessimistic despite widespread tool adoption.
- **Anchor:** "the U.S. is one of the most AI-pessimistic countries in the world. A 2025 Pew poll found five times as many Americans are concerned as are excited"
- **Citation:** (para. 3)
- **Tags:** #trust-fairness, #adoption-barriers

**DP3:** Citizens perceive AI will diminish human cognitive and relational capacities across multiple dimensions.
- **Anchor:** "The public thinks AI will worsen our ability to think creatively, form meaningful relationships, and make difficult decisions"
- **Citation:** (para. 3)
- **Tags:** #skill-formation, #cognitive-offloading

**DP4:** Data center protests have stalled nearly $100 billion in infrastructure investments through grassroots organizing.
- **Anchor:** "From Virginia to Indiana to Arizona, activists stalled $98 billion in data-center projects in the second quarter of 2025 alone"
- **Citation:** (para. 4)
- **Tags:** #policy-gap, #grassroots-adoption-model

**DP5:** Environmental and resource concerns are primary drivers of local AI infrastructure opposition.
- **Anchor:** "fears over data centers' impacts on electricity, water, noise pollution"
- **Citation:** (para. 1)
- **Tags:** #infrastructure, #deployment-bottleneck

**DP6:** Gubernatorial candidates are incorporating AI data-center restrictions as central campaign platform.
- **Anchor:** "Francesca Hong is among a growing contingent of 2026 candidates who are making data-­center restrictions a key campaign promise"
- **Citation:** (p. 7, Francesca Hong section)
- **Tags:** #policy-gap, #regulatory-uncertainty

**DP7:** Residents in data-center counties face measurable increases in electricity costs passed to households and small businesses.
- **Anchor:** "Studies have shown that residents in counties with data centers are facing higher power bills, in part because the cost of upgrades is being passed on to ratepayers"
- **Citation:** (p. 11, Alicia Johnson section)
- **Tags:** #infrastructure, #roi-measurement

**DP8:** Religious leadership is mobilizing faith communities to resist AI implementation on spiritual and psychological grounds.
- **Anchor:** "Pope Leo XIV called out AI in his first address to the College of Cardinals. Its rise, he told the Catholic leaders, required the 'defense of human dignity, justice, and labor'"
- **Citation:** (para. 12, Michael Grayston section)
- **Tags:** #trust-fairness, #workforce-transformation

**DP9:** AI chatbot use among teenagers is reshaping help-seeking patterns away from human relationships and toward automated systems.
- **Anchor:** "half of 13- to 17-year-old respondents talked to AI companions at least a few times a month. Many now consult with bots not only for homework advice, but also for deep moral or philosophical questions"
- **Citation:** (para. 12, Michael Grayston section)
- **Tags:** #skill-formation, #cognitive-offloading

**DP10:** AI-driven cultural production prioritizes volume and speed over artistic originality and creative integrity.
- **Anchor:** "A lot of streaming companies have executives with no background in film who are very focused on generating volume. And AI is really good at doing that faster and cheaper than humans do"
- **Citation:** (p. 19, Justine Bateman section)
- **Tags:** #product-strategy, #measurement-framework-reckoning

**DP11:** AI industry is consolidating corporate monopoly power over critical infrastructure and information access.
- **Anchor:** "if we become too attached to these tools, there's no way to break away from these large monopolies that host them"
- **Citation:** (p. 20, John Palowitch section)
- **Tags:** #competitive-disruption, #saas-repricing

**DP12:** AI systems are being deployed for surveillance applications affecting marginalized populations including Indigenous communities.

- **Citation:** (p. 22, Jordan Harmon & Mackenzie Roberts section)

**DP13:** Indigenous nations are resisting data center projects on grounds of cultural autonomy and resource stewardship.
- **Anchor:** "This feels like a modern-day land run"
- **Citation:** (p. 23, Jordan Harmon & Mackenzie Roberts section)
- **Tags:** #policy-gap, #external-vs-internal-alignment

**DP14:** Tech workers are organizing within and exiting large technology companies citing ethical concerns over dual-use applications.
- **Anchor:** "I gradually became aware that this was dual-use technology, and that it could be used for surveillance, state violence, and genocide"
- **Citation:** (p. 21, John Palowitch section)
- **Tags:** #dual-use-risks, #policy-gap

**DP15:** Conservative populist opposition to AI is framing tech industry accountability as personal autonomy and bodily autonomy issue.

**DP16:** AI implementation in healthcare lacks sufficient safety testing and worker input before deployment.

**DP17:** Nursing professionals report AI tools creating dangerous clinical errors in patient-care coordination.

**DP18:** AI companies are adopting limited guardrails while facing skepticism about enforcement and authenticity of industry self-regulation.
