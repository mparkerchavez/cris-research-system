## Initial Observations

This analysis documents the critical inflection point where AI infrastructure investment justification shifted from theoretical future value to demonstrated present-day demand through agent deployment. The key insight is the transition from training-constrained to inference-constrained economics: previous bear cases assumed a training-infrastructure bubble similar to fiber-optic overbuilding, but underestimated the inference workload multiplier introduced by production agent deployment at scale. The 18-month window for infrastructure dominance creates existential pressure on major tech firms to spend at rates that appear reckless by conventional software company metrics but rational given the competitive stakes and demand signals. The emergence of four distinct human skill categories (taste, domain judgment, learning velocity, honest self-assessment) suggests workforce bifurcation rather than uniform displacement.
