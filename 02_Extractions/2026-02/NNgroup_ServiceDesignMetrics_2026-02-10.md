## Initial Observations

This short video identifies a critical but largely overlooked gap in AI adoption: the collapse of traditional service measurement frameworks when AI becomes a service delivery agent. Key themes include:

1. **Measurement Framework Reckoning**: The speaker articulates four dimensions of metrics that don't exist in current service design frameworks—AI-to-AI performance, human-AI collaboration, data quality for AI, and human trust/control assessment.

2. **AI-to-AI Collaboration Problem**: Unlike human-to-human teams where collaboration metrics exist (synchronization, handoffs, conflict resolution), AI-to-AI system assessment lacks standard metrics despite being essential for agent orchestration services.

3. **Data Quality Elevation**: Data quality becomes a first-class service metric rather than technical infrastructure concern. Bias, representativeness, freshness, and structuring directly impact AI service quality in ways traditional data governance doesn't address.

4. **Trust Asymmetry**: The speaker distinguishes between user trust (comfort with AI task handoff), decision trust (confidence in AI recommendations), and control trust (ability to override/supervise). Current frameworks don't measure these distinctly.

5. **Employee Experience Gap**: Internal-facing AI trust metrics differ from customer-facing metrics. Employee willingness to delegate AND override remain unmeasured, creating blind spots in adoption readiness.

6. **Framework Immaturity Honesty**: The speaker explicitly acknowledges that the technology and measurement approaches are not mature, placing responsibility on service designers to lead definition of success metrics rather than awaiting external standards.

7. **Measurement Precedes Optimization**: The absence of AI-specific metrics means organizations cannot measure, much less optimize, AI-mediated service quality—creating a deployment-bottleneck where organizations scale agents without visibility into actual performance.

This represents a critical governance gap: organizations deploying agents without measurement frameworks for AI-specific performance dimensions, human-AI collaboration quality, or trust/control assessment.
