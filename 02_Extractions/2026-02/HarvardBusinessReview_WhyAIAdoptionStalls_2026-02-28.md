## Data Points

**DP1:** AI adoption failure reflects psychological threat perception, not execution deficiency.
- **Anchor:** "this is not a random failure of execution. It is a predictable psychological pattern"
- **Citation:** (para. 2)
- **Tags:** #adoption-barriers, #ai-angst

**DP2:** Widespread AI deployment coexists with widespread job security anxiety.
- **Anchor:** "88% of companies reporting regular AI use. Yet many leaders report familiar frustrations"
- **Citation:** (para. 1)
- **Tags:** #adoption-barriers, #executive-awareness-gap

**DP3:** One-third of employees experience severe AI-related anxiety across multiple dimensions.
- **Anchor:** "One in three employees had an average score of four or greater across the AI angst composite"
- **Citation:** (para. 3)
- **Tags:** #adoption-barriers, #workforce-readiness

**DP4:** Belief in AI's business value does not correlate with personal security perception.
- **Anchor:** "About 4 in 10 employees strongly believe in AI's business value, while simultaneously fearing what it means for their own security"
- **Citation:** (para. 4)
- **Tags:** #executive-awareness-gap, #trust-fairness

**DP5:** Technology and finance sectors show highest simultaneous optimism and anxiety.
- **Anchor:** "Technology and financial services sit at the center of the optimism-fear tension"
- **Citation:** (para. 8)
- **Tags:** #adoption-barriers, #industry-risk-asymmetry

**DP6:** Professional services face identity-level disruption from AI adoption.
- **Anchor:** "Professional services face perhaps the most identity-disruptive form of AI adoption"
- **Citation:** (para. 12)
- **Tags:** #adoption-barriers, #workforce-transformation

**DP7:** Manufacturing, retail, education show indifference rather than resistance to AI.
- **Anchor:** "In these environments, adoption may stall not because people push back, but because they simply do not feel motivated to engage"
- **Citation:** (para. 7)
- **Tags:** #adoption-barriers, #activation-gap

**DP8:** High anxiety paradoxically increases AI usage while increasing resistance.
- **Anchor:** "Those with high AI angst reported that on average, 65% of their job was currently AI-assisted, compared to 42% of the job for those with low AI angst"
- **Citation:** (para. 10)
- **Tags:** #performance-variance, #activation-gap

**DP9:** Fear-driven usage produces compliance rather than genuine commitment.
- **Anchor:** "Fear about job loss or becoming obsolete can drive compliance and usage, but it does not necessarily produce true buy-in"
- **Citation:** (para. 10)
- **Tags:** #adoption-barriers, #performance-variance

**DP10:** Four distinct employee cohorts require differentiated adoption strategies.
- **Anchor:** "we identified four distinct employee groups based on their beliefs about AI and their anxiety levels"
- **Citation:** (para. 14)
- **Tags:** #adoption-barriers, #workforce-transformation

**DP11:** Visionaries comprise 40% of employees, representing primary peer-adoption leverage.
- **Anchor:** "Visionaries (high belief in its value, low perceived personal risk) see AI's upside and experiment readily. About 40% of employees fall into this group"
- **Citation:** (para. 15)
- **Tags:** #deployment-stages, #workforce-transformation

**DP12:** Usage metrics alone cannot distinguish genuine adoption from anxiety-driven performance.
- **Anchor:** "High usage is often assumed to mean high adoption. In reality, usage can just as easily reflect self-protective compliance"
- **Citation:** (para. 20)
- **Tags:** #roi-measurement, #performance-variance
