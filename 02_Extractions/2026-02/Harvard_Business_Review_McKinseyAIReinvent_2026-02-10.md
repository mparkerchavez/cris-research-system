## Initial Observations

This interview captures an incumbent service provider navigating profound model-driven disruption through three simultaneous transformations:

1. **Workforce composition shift** (40K humans + 20K agents, moving to 1:1 parity in 18 months)—implying massive organizational restructuring and skill reallocation
2. **Business model migration** (33% outcomes-based already, targeting majority)—shifting from advisory (recommendations) to partnership (accountable results)
3. **Hiring profile reorientation** (resilience, teamwork, learning aptitude over credentials)—anticipating future skill composition

The organizational change emphasis (>50% of value realization) counters technology-centric narratives, acknowledging that agentic systems require structural rewiring beyond tool deployment. McKinsey's response to past controversies (OxyContin, South Africa) through governance investment signals recognition that scale and partnership autonomy created opacity risks now requiring explicit controls.

Notable implications:
- Commoditization pressure is real but directional (simpler work shifts to clients/AI; McKinsey chases more complex problems)
- Outcomes-based pricing reduces per-seat dependency vulnerability compared to pure advisory
- Leadership/judgment/aspiration-setting emerge as durable consultant value, though increasingly rare vs. technical delivery
- Partnership structure enables long-term perspective but requires vigilant governance; cultural ethos alone is insufficient

McKinsey's strategic positioning assumes consulting always has clients willing to pay for "doubling market cap"—valid but assumes sustained demand for strategic transformation work across economic cycles.
