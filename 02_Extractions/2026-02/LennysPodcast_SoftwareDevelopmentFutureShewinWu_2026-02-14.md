## Initial Observations

Sherwin Wu's perspective from OpenAI's platform layer provides critical visibility into the organizational transformation driven by agentic systems. The key insight is the shift from development velocity (code per engineer) to management complexity (oversight of agent fleets). The 70% PR increase among heavy Codex users signals that the binding constraint shifts from code generation capacity to code review, testing, and decision-making oversight. The distinction between "what models are today" versus "where they're going" as a product strategy principle suggests that startups building for current capability are optimizing for local maxima while missing exponential capability curves. The emphasis on tiger teams and bottom-up adoption alongside top-down directives mirrors organizational change management literature but suggests AI deployment differs from previous waves (cloud, mobile) in that capability intensity creates steeper adoption curves. The emergence of business process automation as the "biggest untapped opportunity" despite being less discussed in Silicon Valley suggests a market-reality gap that favors pragmatic enterprise vendors over model-developer narratives.
