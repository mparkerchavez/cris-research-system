# Where Senior Leaders Are Struggling with AI

## Data Points

**DP1:** Organizations lack consensus on what constitutes measurable AI value, creating leadership misalignment across stakeholder groups.
- **Anchor:** "One of the biggest challenges for me has been communication around AI, because there are layers to it"
- **Citation:** (para. 39-44)
- **Tags:** #executive-awareness-gap, #roi-measurement, #ai-leadership-readiness

**DP2:** Senior leaders experience continuous disruption from AI requiring sustained change resilience rather than episodic adaptation.
- **Anchor:** "Unlike episodic initiatives, new systems, restructures, or operating-model shifts, AI is causing continual disruption with no clear end point"
- **Citation:** (para. 25)
- **Tags:** #adoption-barriers, #ai-leadership-readiness, #temporal-compression

**DP3:** Rapid organizational change creates leadership credibility risk when strategic direction must be repeatedly revised.
- **Anchor:** "The target's always moving. I know what we said six months ago, but actually the target's moved"
- **Citation:** (para. 27)
- **Tags:** #ai-leadership-readiness, #deployment-bottleneck, #risk-governance

**DP4:** Board and executive pressure to demonstrate AI progress occurs before problems are clearly defined, driven by leadership FOMO.
- **Anchor:** "The biggest challenge isn't resistance, it's leadership FOMO. Everyone wants an update on AI before they understand the problem"
- **Citation:** (para. 48)
- **Tags:** #executive-awareness-gap, #measurement-framework-reckoning, #use-case-development

**DP5:** Narrow ROI framing that requires immediate role elimination discourages employee AI experimentation and idea contribution.
- **Anchor:** "When leadership over-indexes on short-term ROI, people stop bringing forward ideas altogether"
- **Citation:** (para. 52)
- **Tags:** #roi-measurement, #use-case-development, #adoption-barriers

**DP6:** Experienced professionals experience identity threat from AI automation capability, creating resistance that outweighs technical hesitation.
- **Anchor:** "What tends to get in the way isn't the technology itself. A lot of it is about people worrying what this means for their role"
- **Citation:** (para. 63-65)
- **Tags:** #skill-formation, #workforce-transformation, #adoption-barriers

**DP7:** Treating employee fear and skepticism as problems to override reduces engagement rather than accelerating adoption.
- **Anchor:** "Several leaders described early missteps where fear or skepticism was treated as something to be overridden or corrected"
- **Citation:** (para. 67)
- **Tags:** #ai-leadership-readiness, #adoption-barriers, #workforce-readiness

**DP8:** Leaders can normalize AI adoption by visibly experimenting with tools and acknowledging limitations rather than projecting technical expertise.
- **Anchor:** "When senior leaders experimented publicly, sharing prompts, acknowledging limitations, or explaining how outputs were validated, it reframed AI use as learning rather than compliance"
- **Citation:** (para. 96)
- **Tags:** #ai-leadership-readiness, #grassroots-adoption-model, #adoption-barriers

**DP9:** Federated tool access to non-technical functions (HR, legal, finance) generates stronger adoption than top-down technical expertise assumption.
- **Anchor:** "One of the most disruptive things we've done is focus on functions that are usually late to engage with AI. Instead of assuming deep technical expertise is required, we've given teams like HR, legal, and finance tools they can experiment with directly"
- **Citation:** (para. 85)
- **Tags:** #use-case-development, #deployment-stages, #grassroots-adoption-model

**DP10:** Organizations default to pilot activity generation rather than learning without explicit leadership permission to cancel non-performing initiatives.
- **Anchor:** "Without visible leadership ownership of decisions to pause or cancel initiatives, organizations defaulted to running pilots that generated activity but little learning"
- **Citation:** (para. 102)
- **Tags:** #deployment-bottleneck, #measurement-framework-reckoning, #risk-governance

**DP11:** Incremental impact framing (10-30 minutes per day) compounds value but requires redefining ROI beyond headcount reduction.
- **Anchor:** "A tool that helps people write clearer emails or reduces reconciliation time might save 10 or 30 minutes a day. That doesn't cut a role, but it compounds"
- **Citation:** (para. 52)
- **Tags:** #roi-measurement, #workflow-optimization, #productivity-impact

**DP12:** Transparent communication about known unknowns and evolving evidence maintains trust more effectively than false certainty.
- **Anchor:** "Rather than projecting certainty they could not sustain, they focused on consistency: explaining trade-offs, naming risks, and showing how learning informed next steps"
- **Citation:** (para. 117)
- **Tags:** #ai-leadership-readiness, #trust-fairness, #risk-governance
