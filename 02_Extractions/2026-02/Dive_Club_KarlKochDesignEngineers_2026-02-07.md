## Initial Observations

1. **AI Steering Through Documentation:** Karl demonstrates a practical governance approach—feeding AI specific documentation (React's "You Might Not Need an Effect" article) to constrain and guide code generation. This represents a human-in-the-loop strategy that's more nuanced than simple prompting.

2. **The Generalist Swing:** Despite 20+ years of role specialization in tech, Karl identifies a clear reversal driven by AI democratizing code production. This suggests workforce transformation is already underway at design-adjacent roles, not just future-focused scenarios.

3. **Craft as Competitive Differentiation:** In an AI-saturated market, Karl positions craft and intentional detail (animations, micro-interactions, performance) as the human-irreplaceable value. This aligns with themes of creative differentiation in commodity automation contexts.

4. **The "Few Years Off" Inflection Point:** Karl's estimate that full vibe-coding acceptance is "a few years" away (from Feb 2026) positions 2028-2029 as a potential inflection point for hiring and skill requirements. This is notably conservative compared to some AI acceleration narratives.

5. **Debugging as Skill Differentiation:** Karl's emphasis on browser inspector knowledge and direct problem diagnosis (vs. prompt-based solving) suggests that foundational technical literacy remains a high-ROI skill even as AI handles code generation.

6. **Context-Appropriate Restraint:** The discussion of animation philosophy (avoiding spring defaults, matching interaction semantics to UI intent) reveals that good design engineering judgment is about *constraints and context*, not maximizing feature deployment.
