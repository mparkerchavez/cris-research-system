## Initial Observations

Cotra's framework reveals that AGI timeline estimates obscure the more consequential parameter: takeoff speed determines whether AGI leads to marginal economic growth improvement or sci-fi transformation within years. The analysis demonstrates that disagreement on risk is fundamentally not about whether AI becomes superintelligent (most parties accept near-term AGI) but about velocity of subsequent physical technology advancement. Fast takeoff scenarios assume AI R&D automation triggers super-exponential feedback, while slow takeoff scenarios incorporate physical infrastructure bottlenecks (hardware, manufacturing, energy). The no-takeoff position (growth-as-usual) requires denying that automated science produces material civilization advancement benefits. This framework suggests that AI policy should focus less on timeline certainty and more on takeoff speed evidence, as the same timeline can support radically different risk profiles depending on feedback loop assumptions.
