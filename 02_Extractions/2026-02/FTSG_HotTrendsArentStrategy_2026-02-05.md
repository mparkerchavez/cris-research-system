## Initial Observations

This article identifies a fundamental leadership challenge in 2026: organizations are confusing trendy topics (data, privacy, IoT, AI) with actual strategic direction. The author argues that the real problem is not the importance of these trends, but the framework leaders use to engage with them. The core argument is that vision must precede technology adoption, not follow it. Without this sequencing, organizations acquire sophisticated systems that don't support their actual goals. The piece proposes a metaphorical shift—from "buying furniture before designing the house" to establishing vision first, then aligning technologies strategically.

## Key Research Implications

This article positions **#ai-leadership-readiness** as the critical gap, arguing that the problem is not technical capability or trend awareness, but rather the mental models and decision-making frameworks leaders use to engage with emerging technologies. The emphasis on vision-led adoption (rather than trend-led or fear-driven) connects to **#use-case-development** and **#deployment-stages**, suggesting that organizations need clearer frameworks for translating organizational futures into technology selection criteria.

The data-as-a-cost-center insight also strengthens the case for **#data-quality** and **#roi-measurement** as foundational challenges—organizations cannot realize value from data infrastructure without first defining what value they are trying to create.
