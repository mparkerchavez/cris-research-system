## Initial Observations

This transcript provides rare operational detail on what successful enterprise AI adoption looks like in practice. The distinction between "coordinated top-down effort" vs. traditional bottom-up software adoption is a critical insight—it suggests AI requires different change management approaches than previous technology waves.

The "Savile Row prompting" concept is particularly valuable. It's not enough to connect AI to data sources; organizations need deep customization that encodes institutional knowledge, definitions, and thinking patterns. This level of specificity (how numbers appear, how figures are structured) represents substantial investment that may create moats.

The Plan-Delegate-Assess-Compound framework for engineering productivity provides actionable structure. The observation that engineers often skip the planning phase and thus can't compound effectively is a useful diagnostic pattern.

The 85% completion three times before success detail is important for tempering expectations around agentic workflow development. This isn't "prompt and done"—it requires iterative refinement with domain expertise.

The time reduction metrics are striking: 2-3 weeks → 30 minutes for investment memos, 15 hours → 1 hour for project management. These represent order-of-magnitude improvements, not incremental gains.

The "what am I going to do?" question around resistance is insightful. It's not fear of learning AI; it's uncertainty about role identity post-automation. Natalia's answer—building agent relationships like staff management and focusing on higher-value human work—provides a useful reframe.
