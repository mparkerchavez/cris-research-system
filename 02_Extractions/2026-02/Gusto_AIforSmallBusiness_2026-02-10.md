## Initial Observations

The Gusto report presents a mature view of small business AI adoption grounded in quantitative data collection. Key patterns emerge:

**Adoption Reality vs. Expectations:** The 34% non-adoption rate alongside 60% five-year AI expectation suggests significant confidence in technology trajectory, yet current barriers remain substantial.

**Training as Differentiator:** The stark 79%→60% gap between untrained and trained cohorts on productivity gains indicates that simple tool access without upskilling produces minimal benefit. This contradicts the "easy democratization" narrative and points toward #workforce-readiness and #skill-formation as critical success dependencies.

**Strategic Intent Multiplier:** The six-fold difference between tactical and strategic AI use cases suggests organizations require intentional redirection of freed capacity to innovation rather than cost-cutting. This indicates #ai-leadership-readiness as a gatekeeping factor.

**Governance Lagging:** Only 15% policy adoption amid 82% investment suggests regulatory uncertainty and risk-governance gaps. The emphasis on "human touch" preservation reflects concerns about #equity-paradox and trust degradation.

**Agent Curiosity Ahead of Readiness:** 40% agent exploration with only 17% deployment indicates awareness-capability gap. This maps to #deployment-bottleneck and infrastructure readiness questions not addressed in current playbook.
