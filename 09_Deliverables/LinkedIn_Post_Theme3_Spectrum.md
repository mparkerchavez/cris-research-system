This week I have been sharing deeper dives from my February research on AI adoption and workforce transformation. Tuesday was about the skill shift in engineering. [LINK] Wednesday was about the compliance trap hiding inside your usage dashboards. [LINK]

Today: the number that ties it all together.

**68%.**

That is the percentage of knowledge workers Section's AI Proficiency Report (January 2026, 5,000+ respondents) classifies as "experimenters." They can prompt, summarize a meeting, rewrite an email. They have cleared the beginner bar.

And they are stuck.

Section calls it the "use case desert." Experimenters know how to use AI tools but cannot identify meaningful applications in their actual work. **Only 15% have value-driving use cases. Less than 1% build automations.** The gap between knowing how to use AI and knowing what to use it for is where most of the workforce lives.

Companies that have invested in AI training see employees **score an average of 40 out of 100 on proficiency**. Still experimenters. Foundation investments move people from novice to experimenter. They do not solve the leap to practitioner.

That leap requires something different: role-specific use case development, manager support, continuous reinforcement, and time. It is not a knowledge problem. It is an application problem. No amount of general-purpose training will fix it.

The full spectrum: **28% novices, 68% experimenters, 3% practitioners, 0.1% experts.** When ChatGPT launched in November 2022, everyone was a beginner. That moment is gone. People are splintering across a widening spectrum, each moving at a different speed.

At one end, practitioners **saving 8 to 12 hours per week**, fundamentally changing how their function operates. **Practitioners are 20 times more likely to save 4 or more hours per week than novices.** At the other end, **79% of novices cannot protect sensitive information** when using AI, and **86% lack official access to AI tools at work**.

And the proficiency bar keeps rising. What counted as proficient three years ago, writing good prompts, is table stakes now. In 2026, proficiency means workflow integration, real use cases, measurable value. The discipline is evolving from prompt engineering to context engineering to intent engineering, each layer requiring deeper organizational engagement.

Moderna achieved **80% AI proficiency across 5,000 workers**. It took **4 to 5 years**, a dedicated head of AI, and thousands of hands-on sessions. Most organizations are treating this as a quarterly initiative when it requires a multi-year commitment.
