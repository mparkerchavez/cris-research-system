## Summary

Section presented findings from their latest AI proficiency study, surveying over 5,000 knowledge workers in companies with 1,000+ employees across the US, UK, and Canada. The research reveals a significant gap between AI investment and actual workforce proficiency.  

### Study Methodology

- Measures proficiency across three dimensions: usage sophistication, knowledge of brand-safe AI use, and prompting skill
- Participants scored and categorized into four proficiency levels: novice, experimenter, practitioner, and expert
- Focus on actual competency rather than just attitudes or self-reported frequency
- Methodology evolves every six months to reflect current AI capabilities

### Critical Gap Identified

- Companies are investing in AI tools, but only 12% have embedded AI into day-to-day business processes
- 56% of knowledge workers use AI at least weekly, but only 3% are truly proficient
- When filtering for meaningful work use, only 15% have valuable work use cases, and less than 1% build automations
- 15% of all adults worldwide now use ChatGPT weekly, showing massive consumer adoption

### Proficiency Level Breakdown

**AI Novices (28% of workforce)** 

- Rarely or never use AI for work, poor prompting skills, don't understand AI's capabilities or risks
- 57% don't know major LLM risks, 70% don't understand core capabilities, 79% don't know how to protect sensitive information
- 86% have no official access to AI at work, 88% haven't been trained, 82% say their manager discourages or is silent on AI use
- Represent a net risk to organizations, not just neutral

**AI Experimenters (69% of workforce)** 

- Use AI frequently and can prompt decently, but lack meaningful work use cases
- Most common uses: Google replacement, grammar correction, email summarization
- Stuck in a "use case desert" - know how to use AI but don't know what to use it for
- Many get value from AI in personal life (grocery lists, bedtime stories) but not for work
- Even senior roles like HR directors, customer service VPs, and engineering managers don't believe AI has a significant role in their work

**AI Practitioners (3% of workforce)** 

- Execute intermediate to advanced use cases beyond summarization
- Top use cases: analyzing data for insights, generating reports, competitive intelligence, strategic thought partnership, content generation
- 77% save more than 4 hours per week (roughly 10% of their time)
- 85% report AI has significantly or transformatively benefited their work

**AI Experts (less than 1% of workforce)** 

- Build repeatable AI solutions and automations that others can use
- Create custom GPTs, automated workflows, and simple agents
- Organizations should aim for 5-10% of workforce at this level

### Function-Specific Findings

Half of engineers aren't using AI to write or debug code, more than half of marketers aren't using it for first drafts, and most product people aren't using it to create prototypes  

### Time Savings Reality

- Majority not reporting meaningful time savings yet
- Organizations seeing measurable ROI report employee time savings of 8-12 hours per week
- Current basic use cases (summarization, Google replacement) only save 1-2 hours per week

### Executive-Employee Perception Gap

Executives are significantly out of touch with workforce reality: 

- 50% of C-level think their organization has widespread AI adoption vs. 26% of managers and below
- 76% of C-level say company encourages experimentation vs. 25% of managers and below
- Wide gaps exist across all measures including tool access and policy clarity
- More senior employees receive more support from managers; IC-level support drops significantly

### Foundation Gaps

Many companies haven't implemented basics: 

- One-third of workers report no AI policy
- Only half say they have universal access to tools
- Less than half have received AI training
- Only one-third report a company-wide AI strategy exists

### Impact of Foundation Investments

Companies with AI strategy, tools, and training show higher proficiency scores than those without  

However, these foundational investments only move employees from novice to experimenter - they don't solve the use case desert   

### Emotional State of Workforce

More junior employees report feeling anxious and overwhelmed about AI versus excited senior counterparts  

Average employee is more anxious than excited, indicating a human problem, not just a technical one 

### Benchmark for Success

World-class AI-powered organization should aim for: 

- 80% proficiency overall
- 70% at practitioner level
- 10% at expert level
- 15-20% experimenters (no more novices)

Moderna cited as example: 80% proficiency across 5,000 workers, 100% monthly usage, 66% weekly usage, achieved over 4-5 years with dedicated head of AI and appropriate budget    

### Recommendations for Leaders

**Immediate priorities:**

- Solve the use case desert - highest leverage action
- Focus on moving experimenters to practitioners
- Don't be delusional about your organization's actual status

**Leadership behaviors:**    

- Find and implement advanced AI use cases relevant to your function
- Use AI as a thought partner daily
- Model and expect use case sharing across the organization
- Reward high-value use cases with incentives
- Embed AI in operating principles and review processes

**Mindset and approach:**   

- Recognize this is very hard - proficiency bar will keep rising
- Lead from the front with transparency and empathy
- Commit to sustained effort (not one-time training)
- Make it a daily habit and expectation
- Expect this to take significant time (potentially a full year to move 60% from experimenter to practitioner)

### Use Case Library Best Practices

Effective use case libraries must include:  

- Clear objective and problem being solved
- Expected value/outcomes (not just ROI)
- Step-by-step implementation guide (prompts, custom GPTs, agents)
- Actual results achieved
- Usage frequency
- Tips, tricks, and screen recordings
- Access to the person who created it

Cannot be just a prompt library - must inspire, consolidate, and guide  

Requires launch event with hands-on keyboard time and continuous reinforcement
