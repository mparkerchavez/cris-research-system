## IDEA 4: Core Capability Endures; Expression Is Context-Dependent

### Thesis
Fundamental human capability (pattern recognition, judgment, learning agility) is durable across contexts, BUT market expression is highly volatile. Bimodal outcome: high-endurance skills (taste, judgment, specification) show >85% portability; context-specific execution skills show <50% portability and decreasing value. "Skill elevation treadmill" paradox. Protective counterforce: "productive resistance."

### Open Questions (Evidence-Informed)

1. **Bifurcation Point Identification:** Are September 2025 (42% deployment) or December 2025 (capability jump) the actual bifurcation points? What metrics distinguish "stalled pilots" from "accelerating loops"?

2. **Capability Overhang Duration:** Frontier models beat humans 74% (Dec 2025); mainstream users still at ChatGPT 3.5/4. How long does this adoption gap persist? Does it follow S-curve (closing) or power law (widening)?

3. **Complexity Barrier Nature:** Why did complexity double Q1→Q3 when deployment grew? Are organizations discovering technical difficulty (integration, governance, data) or capability-expectation mismatch?
