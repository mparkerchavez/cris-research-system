## Key Takeaways

**EVIDENCE DENSITY REQUIREMENTS:**
- **MINIMUM:** Each takeaway MUST cite 3-5+ sources
- **TARGET:** 5-7 citations per takeaway for major claims
- **AVOID:** Single-source claims; always show convergence across multiple sources
- **FORMAT:** Embed citations throughout the narrative, not just at the end

**Structure per Takeaway:**
- Bold title stating the core thesis
- 2-4 sentences of elaboration with citations embedded throughout
- Show the evidence chain, not just the conclusion
- Each takeaway should tell a complete story — the finding, why it matters, and what it implies

**Organization:**
- Organize by insight, not by tag or source
- Each takeaway should represent a distinct position, not just a restated finding
- Aim for 8-12 takeaways total

**Example of Proper Evidence Density:**

**1. Agentic AI Breaks Employment Taxonomy; Duality Creates Operational Leverage**

Agentic AI systems exhibit simultaneously tool-like and worker-like properties, forcing organizations to resolve four distinct operational tensions [BCG_EmergingAgenticEnterprise DP2, DP5, DP22]. Seventy-six percent of users experience agentic systems as collaborative coworkers rather than tools [BCG DP2], with ninety-five percent job satisfaction when embracing this duality [BCG DP22; McKinsey_AgentsRobotsSkillPartnerships DP14]. Traditional management structures assume binary classification (tool or worker) [McKinsey DP7; Anthropic_ClaudesConstitution DP11], but agentic systems force organizations to manage ambiguity across responsibility allocation, capability attribution, outcome ownership, and error liability [BCG DP5; Google_DeepMind_ScienceOfScalingAgents DP14]. Organizations that acknowledge this duality as first-order rather than exceptional condition achieve higher deployment velocity and user satisfaction [BCG DP22; McKinsey DP14].

## Template Notes

**Purpose:** Current Synthesis is the "home page" of the CRIS system — a cumulative position statement that answers "where does our understanding stand right now?"

**Distinction from Weekly Learnings:** Weekly tracks *movement* (what changed). Current Synthesis captures *position* (where we've landed). Weekly is organized by tag; Current Synthesis is organized by insight.

**Distinction from Monthly Synthesis:** Monthly is a time-bounded narrative rollup of a specific month. Current Synthesis is always-current and gets overwritten (with archive).

**Archiving:** Before each update, copy the current version to `06_Current_Understanding/archive/Current_Synthesis_YYYY-MM-DD.md`.

**When to update:** After each Weekly Synthesis workflow completes. Update cadence matches Weekly Learnings cadence.

**What to load:** Active_Ideas.md, the latest Weekly Learnings, User_Observations.md, and _Index.md.

**Citation contract:** Every bracket citation should include one `<!-- cite:... -->` metadata block per source. See `_System/Citation_Contract.md`.
