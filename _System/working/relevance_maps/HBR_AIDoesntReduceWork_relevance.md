## Key Synthesis Notes

### High Alignment to Active Ideas
This HBR article provides the empirical grounding for Ideas #3 (Multi-Dimensional Chasm), #10 (Observability Imperative), and #11 (Data Quality). The 8-month case study at a 200-person tech company demonstrates:

1. **Perception Gap (53-point mentioned in Idea #3):** Workers feel more productive (DP15) yet objective workload intensified (DP3, DP14)
2. **Measurement Framework Failure:** Organizations cannot distinguish productivity gains from unsustainable intensity (DP17)
3. **Data Quality Decline:** Human capital quality (judgment, decision-making, cognitive capacity) deteriorates while output metrics improve (DP4, DP18)

### Critical Bifurcation Signals (Idea #6)
The article's proposed "AI practice" framework (DPs 20-24) represents the leadership choice: organizations that intentionally design governance structures (pauses, sequencing, human grounding) will preserve cognitive capacity; those that celebrate "productivity" gains without governance will drift toward unsustainable burnout cycles.

### Understudied Dimension: UI-Governance Coupling
The article reveals a design failure point: conversational interfaces reduce friction to work initiation (DPs 8-9), enabling boundary erosion without organizational governance counterweight. This suggests **specification must precede affordance design** — the "easy prompting" that makes AI adoption feel successful creates technical debt in governance architecture.

### Timeline Compression Signal
The 8-month timeframe shows self-acceleration embedded in culture rapidly (DP13). If this represents 3-year normalization compressed to 4-5 months (consistent with 50% timeline compression observed elsewhere), this suggests Enterprise Timeline Compression thread operating at observed rate.
