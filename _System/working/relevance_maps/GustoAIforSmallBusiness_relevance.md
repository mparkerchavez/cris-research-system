## Synthesis Notes

This source provides **empirical quantification of the multi-dimensional implementation chasm** through small business data. Key findings:

1. **Massive perception-to-action gap** (67% aware / 15% regular) = 52-point chasm validates idea #3
2. **Training is 3x multiplier** (79% no-gain to 60% 20%+ gain) = skill layer is decisive
3. **Strategy multiplies outcomes 6x** (tactical vs. strategic) = design value proven empirically
4. **Governance gap** (82% using / 15% with policy) = observability deficiency
5. **Agent awareness exceeds deployment 2.4x** (40% exploring / 17% deployed) = execution gap
6. **Early adopters compound advantage** (gains + revenue + innovation) = bifurcation is accelerating

The Small Business Adoption Puzzle is particularly important: unlike enterprise (where cost/budget/contracts matter), SMB barriers are skill/confidence/culture. This suggests:

- **Small businesses cannot be sold into AI** (cost-cutting pitch doesn't overcome skill barrier)
- **Require hands-on experiential learning** (51% learn by doing; passive methods fail)
- **Need design framework** (strategy/roadmap 2x multiplier proves structure matters)
- **Risk of failed implementations** (40% exploring agents, only 17% deployed = high failure rate)

The transparency benefit (DP25-26) is an underappreciated systemic advantage: AI adoption forces process documentation, enables visibility, drives alignment as co-benefit. This suggests that governance/culture improvements are outcome of AI implementation, not prerequisite.

The temporal implication: small businesses showing 15% regular adoption (late 2025 data) suggests bifurcation is occurring NOW, not waiting for 2026. Those 15% will compound advantage faster than 67% can close gap in next 12 months.
