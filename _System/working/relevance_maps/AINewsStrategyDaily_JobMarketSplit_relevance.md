## Section 6: SYNTHETIC INSIGHTS & NARRATIVE THREADS

### Thread A: Specification as Strategic Bottleneck (DP1-DP7, DP13, DP15-DP16)
This source documents a major inversion: implementation was historically the constraint; specification is now irreducibly human. The source frames this not as opportunity but as *organizational crisis*—most organizations have no discipline around specification, and the incentives that created specification discipline are now disappearing (because implementation is free). **Implication:** 2026 competitive advantage goes to orgs that can *institutionalize* specification discipline despite economic incentives working against it.

### Thread B: The Judgment Economy (DP8, DP12, DP21-DP22)
Economic value concentrates on "output per unit of human judgment"—a learnable skill creating the bifurcation. This is fundamentally different from other technological disruptions (where jobs disappear; here they transform). **Implication:** The bifurcation is *not inevitable*; it's a choice point. Leadership that builds judgment skills across 70-80% of workforce prevents split; those that don't create two-tier economy.

### Thread C: Temporal Compression as Competitive Wedge (DP18, DP4, DP9)
18-24 month J-curve compression creates a narrow window where early adopters (spec-native, agent-orchestrating orgs) achieve 10x-80x revenue multiples over slower adopters. Parallel: AI-native companies (Midjourney, Lovable, Cursor) compress 10-year company building into product-market fit at massive scale. **Implication:** Speed of organizational transformation (not quality of transformation) drives competitive outcome.

### Thread D: Historical Displacement Pattern with Lag (DP19-DP20)
Telephone operator parallel: occupational disruption doesn't kill jobs overnight but routes workers to lower-paying roles or exits them. SaaS market disruption ($285B) shows vendor displacement accelerating. **Implication:** Worker displacement risk is moderate timeline (5-10 years) with concentrated impact on entry-level and junior roles; high-judgment roles grow.
