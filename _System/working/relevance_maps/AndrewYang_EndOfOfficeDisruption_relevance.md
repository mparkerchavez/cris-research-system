## (6) Critical Patterns

### Pattern 1: Cascading Displacement (High Priority)
**Mechanism:** Office automation → Service economy collapse → CRE devaluation → Municipal fiscal crisis → Household liquidity
**Evidence:** DP5, DP9, DP13
**Strategic Implication:** Disruption is not isolated to white-collar workers. Cascades into broader economic instability. Single-intervention points (e.g., job retraining) insufficient without addressing secondary collapse.

### Pattern 2: Phased Extraction (High Priority)
**Mechanism:** Coordinated workforce reduction in waves (DP2: "15% now, 20% in 2 years") maximizes financial extraction while minimizing coordination burden
**Evidence:** DP2, DP14
**Strategic Implication:** Supports Active Idea #2 (Coordination Tax). Phased approach is deliberate strategy, not constrained by technology. Suggests governance intervention window is narrowing (per DP15).

### Pattern 3: Credential-Opportunity Decoupling (High Priority)
**Mechanism:** College costs remain high; employment outcomes for graduates collapse (52% underemployment). Education premium shrinks while debt burden grows.
**Evidence:** DP7, DP8
**Strategic Implication:** Bimodal institutional outcomes: marginal colleges close, elite institutions survive. Creates debt trap for marginal students. Generalist skills may return (Open Thread #7).

### Pattern 4: Domain-Verification Correlation (Moderate Priority)
**Mechanism:** High-clarity domains (legal, finance, marketing) see immediate expert system deployment. Verification clarity predicts adoption velocity.
**Evidence:** DP12
**Strategic Implication:** Connects to Active Idea #15 (Verification Clarity as Domain Disruption Predictor). Legal and financial information processing highly codifiable; design less so. Suggests timing framework for domain-specific disruption.

### Pattern 5: Social Contract Collapse (High Priority for Active Ideas Refresh)
**Mechanism:** Accumulated disruption without safety net redesign creates sustained political unrest. "Study hard, go to school, get good job, live decent life" contract vaporized.
**Evidence:** DP10, DP16
**Strategic Implication:** Active Idea #9 (Trust Multiplier & Authenticity Crisis) strengthened. Emotional/behavioral consequences (despair, addiction, family instability) are not downstream effects but central to disruption dynamics. Trust crisis is not primarily technical but existential.

### Pattern 6: Bimodal Labor Market (Moderate Priority)
**Mechanism:** Middle managers most vulnerable (salary-to-value compression). Displaced workers accept 80%+ pay cuts for part-time roles.
**Evidence:** DP3, DP4
**Strategic Implication:** Not single-tier displacement but stratified by existing position and leverage. Supports Active Idea #16 (Cost Accessibility Stratification). Wage floors may not exist as mechanisms.
