## Section 3: Contradictions & Tensions

DPs that reveal friction between Active Ideas or challenge assumptions.

### Tension A: Viral Paradox (Idea #7 vs Idea #13)
- **Contradiction:** Agent-native products generate "virtually infinite use cases" (non-linear, unpredictable), suggesting massive addressable market. Yet source argues against virality, suggesting limited market.
- **Implication:** Market size is large but fragmented (infinite niches); traditional viral metrics misfit because customers cluster in domain-specific groups, not mass market
- **Relevant DPs:** DP3, DP12, DP10
- **Resolution Path:** Success metric should be "deep penetration in domain" not "mass adoption"; reshape growth narrative from viral to embedded

### Tension B: Feedback Quality Inversion (Idea #13 vs OT5)
- **Contradiction:** Work intensification suggests "more user engagement", but rapid viral growth actually damages feedback quality; slow, deep engagement improves learning
- **Implication:** Counterintuitive: quality metrics improve when growth slows; inverse relationship between adoption speed and data quality
- **Relevant DPs:** DP1, DP2
- **Resolution Path:** Pre-activation strategy is quality filter; select for engaged users (higher data quality) rather than maximize raw volume

### Tension C: Trust Paradox (Idea #9 vs Industry Norms)
- **Contradiction:** Building trust requires personal, direct communication (DP7); but scalable business models typically depersonalize relationships at scale
- **Implication:** Agent products may not follow traditional SaaS scaling curves; personal engagement might be permanent cost, not startup phase
- **Relevant DPs:** DP7, DP8
- **Resolution Path:** Agent products might be fundamentally less scalable than software; success criteria should be "sustainable domain leadership" not "scale to billions"

### Tension D: Specification Paradox (Idea #3 vs Idea #7)
- **Contradiction:** Traditional software metrics fail (DP6) because agent value is unmeasurable/contextual. Yet pre-activation (DP9) requires clear specification of capabilities to users
- **Implication:** Agents are simultaneously "indefinably valuable" (qualitative) and "must be clearly specified" (verifiable); contradiction is real
- **Relevant DPs:** DP3, DP6, DP9
- **Resolution Path:** Specification happens at interaction level, not feature level; users learn to ask right questions, not developers define outputs
