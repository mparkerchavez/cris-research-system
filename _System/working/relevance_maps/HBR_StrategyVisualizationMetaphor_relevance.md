## Section 1: DPs Directly Relevant to Active Ideas

### Idea 1: Tool-Coworker Duality (Work Intensification Dimension)

**Relevance:** STRONG - Addresses the perception gap between leadership narrative and worker experience through metaphor/storytelling mechanism.

**Connected DPs:**

**DP1:** Strategy execution success requires employee emotional engagement, not just cognitive clarity
- **Anchor:** "metaphor and storytelling did not change what people understood; they changed how much they cared"
- **Why relevant:** Directly validates the work intensification paradox. Organizations communicate cost reduction narratives (cognitive clarity) that fail to emotionally engage workers who experience actual burden. The HBR research shows metaphors bypass this by appealing to emotional commitment rather than rational comprehension.
- **Mechanism:** The duality thesis posits workers treat agents as tools while executives anticipate cost reduction. HBR shows this gap persists even when strategy is intellectually clear—the missing piece is emotional alignment.

**DP2:** Metaphor-based strategy visualizations significantly increase employee engagement compared to abstract diagrams
- **Anchor:** "participants exposed to the metaphor-based visualization reported significantly higher engagement"
- **Why relevant:** Quantifies the engagement mechanism. If 95% of extensive agentic adopters report job satisfaction (Active Idea 1), understanding how to communicate agentic transformation to create that emotional alignment is critical.

**DP3:** Visual metaphors and storytelling drive strategy commitment without improving comprehension of the underlying strategy itself
- **Anchor:** "there were no meaningful differences in how the strategy itself was perceived"
- **Why relevant:** KEY FINDING for the perception gap. Engagement and commitment operate independently from understanding. This suggests the cost-reduction/burden gap persists because the *strategy understanding* is identical in both groups—what differs is emotional buy-in. HBR provides the mechanism: use metaphor to create commitment despite unchanged comprehension.

### Idea 7: Agent-Native Development (Design and Communication Frameworks)

**Relevance:** MODERATE-STRONG - Provides methodology for communicating agent transformation to workforce, independent of technical implementation.

**Connected DPs:**

**DP4:** Effective strategy metaphors must pass the "4 Fs test": Fitting, Feeling familiar yet fresh, and Facilitating understanding
- **Anchor:** "a successful visual strategy metaphor has to pass what we call the 4 Fs test"
- **Why relevant:** Active Idea 7 addresses "the adoption of agent-native paradigms by organizations and individuals." The 4 Fs framework is a testable, replicable methodology for communicating agent-native transformation. Organizations adopting agents need communication frameworks—this provides one.

**DP5:** Metaphor fit requires alignment with industry, culture, and leadership style rather than universal applicability
- **Anchor:** "metaphors should be related not only to the strategy itself but also to the organization's industry, culture, and leadership style"
- **Why relevant:** Supports the "culture-specific implementation" dimension of agent-native adoption. No single metaphor works universally; each organization must adapt based on existing culture, industry norms, and leadership orientation.

### Open Thread 3: Multi-Agent Complexity (Understanding coordination costs)

**Relevance:** WEAK-INDIRECT - Addresses communication complexity, not coordination complexity, but both are systems thinking problems.

**Connected DPs:**

**DP7:** Strategy visualizations operate at two levels: cognitive clarity and emotional resonance through narrative depth
- **Anchor:** "The first level focuses on clarity: making strategic elements visible, structured, and logically connected. The second level builds on this foundation by adding emotional and narrative depth"
- **Why relevant:** Open Thread 3 explores why multi-agent systems create complexity problems. HBR's two-level framework (cognitive + emotional) parallels the complexity problem: organizations can achieve cognitive clarity on agent coordination but struggle with emotional/organizational resonance. Both require narrative depth to resolve.

**DPs:** 4, 5, 9, 10

- **Anchor:** "Apply the metaphor consistently and frequently in indirect and direct leadership communication"
