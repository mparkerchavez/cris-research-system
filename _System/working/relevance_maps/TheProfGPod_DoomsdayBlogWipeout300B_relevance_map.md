## Section 4: NEW PATTERNS - Themes Not Covered by Active Ideas

### Pattern A: Sentiment-Driven Pricing Dominates in AI-Uncertainty Conditions
**Strength:** Novel, evidence-dense
**Source DPs:** 2, 3, 15, 18, 19, 24, 25

**What It Reveals:**
When fundamental economic models for AI disruption don't exist, market repricing becomes emotion-driven rather than information-driven. The $300B selloff from a blog post with zero new information demonstrates that in high-uncertainty domains, narrative quality matters more than information quality.

**Implication:**
This suggests a new research angle: **Narrative Efficiency in Uncertainty Domains.** Under what conditions does narrative structure trump fundamental analysis? When fundamental models exist (traditional corporate finance), narrative has limited repricing power. When fundamental models are absent (AI economic impact), narrative becomes the price-discovery mechanism.

**Potential New Idea:** "Information Asymmetry Creates Narrative Dependency" - markets default to narrative-based pricing when fundamental models are unavailable, creating volatility that signals framework absence rather than genuine risk.

**Strength:** High, specific mechanisms described
**Source DPs:** 11, 12, 13, 14

**What It Reveals:**

**Implication:**

**Connection to Existing Ideas:** Closely related to Idea 13 (Infrastructure Divergence) but extends into financial system design. This suggests Infrastructure Divergence is not just about AI deployment but about any divergent infrastructure maintaining opacity advantages.

### Pattern C: Friction Elimination Creates Value Transfer, Not Value Destruction
**Strength:** Moderate, validated by historical example
**Source DPs:** 5, 6, 17

**What It Reveals:**
The Visa precedent (DP17) shows friction elimination typically creates new businesses rather than economic elimination. Check-writing friction didn't disappear; it transferred to credit card infrastructure, creating massive new economic value. This validates the core counterargument to doomsday narratives.

**Implication:**
This pattern appears in: traditional tech disruption (Schumpeter's creative destruction), agentic AI workflow transformation (constraints shifted, not eliminated), and financial system evolution (friction transfers to new infrastructure). The consistent pattern suggests: **Technology Disruption = Friction Transfer Economics** rather than pure value destruction.

**Opportunity:** This could strengthen positioning for countering doomsday narratives. Not "AI won't disrupt work" but "AI will transform work through friction transfer, creating new economic categories while eliminating old friction."
