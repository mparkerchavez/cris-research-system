## TP1: "The Time Savings Narrative Is Dead. Output Multiplication Is the Real Game."

Heavy users don't prioritize time savings at all. They prioritize output multiplication. This isn't a measurement problem or a communication problem. It's a fundamental shift in how organizations are actually using AI. The game changed, and most executives haven't noticed yet. Organizations that are still selling AI on efficiency will be disappointed. Organizations betting on capability multiplication will win.

### Why It Matters
**Business:** If your AI investment narrative is built on cost reduction and time savings, you're optimizing for the wrong outcome. Your actual users are multiplying output capacity, taking on bigger problems, and expanding scope. The value comes from doing more, not from doing the same thing faster. This changes the business case entirely. Cost reduction through efficiency is temporary. Value multiplication through capability expansion is sustainable and compounds.

**Strategic:** The organizations that acknowledge this shift and design their AI deployments to enable output multiplication will extract asymmetric value. They'll create new capabilities, expand market opportunities, and position their teams as force multipliers. The organizations that optimize for time savings will find their people absorbed into new problems because the real value isn't speed, it's scope.

## TP3: "The Abduction Gap Protects Your Best People. But Only If You Know What It Is."

### The Point
DeepMind research reveals a structural limitation in large language models: they master induction and deduction but systematically fail at abductive reasoning. Induction is learning from examples. Deduction is logical reasoning. Abduction is inferring the best explanation from incomplete information. This isn't a scale issue. It's an architectural ceiling. This matters because abductive reasoning is where human judgment lives. It's the capability that experienced practitioners use to solve novel problems, navigate ambiguity, and make decisions with incomplete data. The domains where AI faces the most disruption pressure are the domains where abductive reasoning is least critical. The domains where human judgment is most valuable are the domains where AI has the hardest structural limit. If your organization knows this, you can use it strategically. Your best people aren't at risk. They're becoming more valuable. But you have to understand the abduction gap to see it.

### Why It Matters
**Business:** Disruption pressure isn't uniform across all domains. It concentrates where verification is clear (engineering arrives first, finance follows, legal/customer service later). Organizations that understand this can invest defensively in high-abduction domains and invest aggressively in automation in low-abduction domains. This prevents overestimating disruption risk and underestimating opportunity.

**Strategic:** The organizations that understand the abduction gap will position experienced practitioners as irreplaceable orchestrators of agent teams. Instead of asking "which jobs will AI take?" the question becomes "which capabilities require abduction?" That reframe turns the threat into an advantage. Experienced practitioners don't get displaced. They get leveraged across agent teams, and their value multiplies.

| **AI Strategy Lead** | Portfolio prioritization | "Disruption doesn't arrive everywhere at once. It follows the abduction requirement curve. Where does your portfolio sit on that curve?" |
